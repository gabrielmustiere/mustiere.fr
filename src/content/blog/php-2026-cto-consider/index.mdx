Fourteen years writing PHP. About fifty projects shipped since 2012 — from the Sylius e-commerce that's been running for three years, to the B2B API rebuilt on
API Platform, to the SaaS migrated to FrankenPHP worker mode this year. The constant thread: Symfony.

This article is not a comparison or a response to another ecosystem. It's what I see today, in 2026, from the inside: a language that has become strict and
fast, frameworks that now cover the full spectrum — APIs, SaaS, e-commerce, back-office, real-time, mobile, AI — tooling that has gone industrial, and
infrastructure that has quietly erased the historical performance argument.

I'm writing this for CTOs and tech leads who want an up-to-date map of the PHP ecosystem, and for PHP developers who occasionally need to step back and look at
what their language now allows. This is field feedback, not advocacy.
