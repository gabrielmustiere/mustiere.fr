There's a very telling moment when you take a RAG from demo to production: you realize the embedding model was the easy problem. Everything else — chunking, ingestion, business filtering, re-ranking, evaluation — is patient plumbing work that nobody sells on LinkedIn.

## Where it breaks, in order

**Chunking.** The first enemy. Splitting by paragraph gives you incomplete context; splitting by page mashes unrelated ideas together. The right split depends on the corpus, not the model. Plan to iterate on it.

**Metadata.** A RAG without `tenant_id`, `date`, or `document_type` filters never leaves the PoC. Add them in v0 — it's cheaper than retrofitting.

**Re-ranking.** Pure vector similarity ranks badly past the top-3. A lightweight cross-encoder on the top-20 improves results more than swapping in a better embedding ever will.

**Evaluation.** Without an eval suite anchored in real cases, you have no idea whether your latest change made things better or worse. See my other piece on LLM evals.

## Priority order

Prototype → decent chunking → metadata → re-ranking → better embeddings → latency optimization. In that order. Swapping embedding models before you've handled the first three floors is almost always motion without progress.
