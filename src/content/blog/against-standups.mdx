The daily standup has become an unchallenged reflex in most engineering teams. Yet when you actually measure what it produces, the payoff is thin: dependencies are either already known, or surfaced too late.

## Four signals that it's no longer doing its job

- More than half the participants aren't listening — they're coding.
- The real blockers get resolved after the standup, in 1-1s.
- "Yesterday / today / blockers" gets read mechanically, with no follow-up question.
- The actual duration consistently overshoots the scheduled one.

If you tick three out of four, the ritual is dead. Either evolve it or kill it.

## What works better

For a team that knows each other (> 3 months working together): a daily async thread in a dedicated channel. Everyone posts what's blocking them, nothing else. Zero "I worked on." Others read when they come online.

For a new team: a real 45-minute weekly sync, with demo and decisions, instead of five shallow standups.

The energy you get back by killing the daily ritual more than pays for the "coordination" it was supposedly providing.
