Two years ago, adding vector search to an app meant standing up Pinecone, Weaviate, or Qdrant next to Postgres. Today, a lot of projects start with `CREATE EXTENSION vector;` and stop right there. That's usually the right call. Sometimes it isn't.

## What pgvector does really well

For corpora under 1 to 5 million embeddings, with dimensions ≤ 1536, pgvector is remarkable:

- **ACID transactions** on embeddings: you write a document and its vector in the same transaction.
- **Metadata filtering** through your existing B-tree indexes, in plain SQL.
- **One system** to operate, back up, and upgrade.
- **Cost** stays whatever your current Postgres bill is.

The HNSW index shipped in 0.5 brings query times down to a few milliseconds on several million rows, provided you have enough RAM.

## Where it hurts

I've watched four projects hit the same walls:

1. **Vector index combined with selective filters.** HNSW is approximate; it doesn't compose gracefully with a `WHERE tenant_id = ?` that wipes out 99% of the rows. The planner has to choose between vector index + re-filtering, or filtered scan + sort by distance. Both plans scale poorly past ~50M rows.
2. **Index maintenance.** Rebuilding HNSW is expensive at scale. In multi-tenant setups with heavy inserts, this becomes a real operational problem.
3. **Memory.** HNSW wants to live in RAM. On a Postgres already working hard on transactional load, you end up arbitrating between hot-table cache and vector index. Rarely comfortable.
4. **Re-ranking.** Dedicated vector databases expose hybrid re-ranking primitives (BM25 + vector + filters) that are more ergonomic than stitching it together in SQL.

## The rule I give teams

Start with pgvector. Always. Leave Postgres only when one of these three thresholds gets crossed:

- Volume > 10M embeddings **and** a < 50ms p95 latency requirement.
- More than 3 selective filters to combine with vector similarity.
- You need native BM25 + vector search in the same engine.

Otherwise, the most useful answer for the client is: "we stay on Postgres, we tune the HNSW index, and we add RAM before we add another system."
